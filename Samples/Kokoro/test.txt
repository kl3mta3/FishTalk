They had not seen the light of a star in what their instruments measured as nearly a hundred billion years of time relative to them. The ship moved through the dark at a speed that made the word "speed" feel inadequate,  leaving light itself concerned it might lose some galactic race. Outside, the universe aged at what seems almost instantaneously while inside, a Tuesday passed. Not to be confused with the day that follows a Sol-Earth Monday, this is a mere coincidence, and in fact it is more realistically closer to a Sol-Earth Saturday than a Tuesday. This is a real problem for many advanced Civilizations and has led to the destruction of an entire Galaxy when the Genaxions thought they were stood-up and vaporized an entire 200 lightyears of space. The Genaxions were quite embarrassed to find out they actually showed up two days early. Since then the Council of Universal Law requires express explanation of any Tuesday mentioned to avoid repeat awkwardness.
